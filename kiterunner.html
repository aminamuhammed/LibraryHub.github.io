The Kite Runner

The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini is a 2003 novel that follows the story of Amir, a young boy from Kabul, Afghanistan, during the Soviet invasion of 1978. The story is set against the backdrop of Afghanistan's history, from the fall of the monarchy to the rise of the Taliban regime, and explores themes of guilt, redemption, and the effects of childhood experiences.

The Kite Runner has become a beloved classic of contemporary literature, touching millions of readers and launching Hosseini's career as one of America's most treasured writers. In 2007, it was adapted into a Hollywood film of the same name.
